Oil Spill Detection Methods

Satellite Imaging Satellite-based monitoring systems are used to detect the extent and location of oil spills on the surface of the water.
Aerial Surveillance Aerial imagery from drones or helicopters helps in quickly detecting oil spills in remote or large bodies of water.
Infrared Sensors Infrared technology can detect the temperature differences between oil and water, aiding in oil spill detection.
Sonar Technology Underwater sonar systems are used to detect oil spills beneath the water's surface, especially in deep-sea environments.
Visual Colorimetry Colorimetric sensors can detect specific chemical signatures of oil on the water's surface, providing accurate readings of contamination levels.
Oil Spill Recovery Tools Advanced tools, such as skimmers and booms, are deployed in response to detected oil spills to prevent further contamination.
